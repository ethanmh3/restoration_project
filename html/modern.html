Modern Revelation

Modern Prophets and Apostles

          Today, we still have God's chosen servants who lead and direct His Church through revelation. The revelation that they receive helps to lead the Church as the circumstances in the world continue to change. They make changes in policy and some practices as necessary to adapt to current circumstances or as inspired by God. They never make changes to core, foundational doctrines that God has established.

General Conference

          Every six months, we have the opportunity to listen to modern prophets, apostles, and other leaders of the Church through a worldwide meeting of the Church known as General Conference. At this conference, leaders of the Church choose specific topics to speak on which they feel would be most beneficial to build the faith, strength, hope, and testimony of all those listening. All are invited to participate in General Conference to hear these uplifting messages and learn more about Jesus Christ.

Proclamations

          Every once in a while, the First Presidency and the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles receive revelation to proclaim statements of crucial doctrine relating to the beliefs and practices of the Church. These documents are fundamental in purpose and a method to declare to the world what God would have it know.

  • The Living Christ
  • Testifies of Jesus Christ as the Savior of the World and the central figure of the Plan of Salvation
  • The Family: A Proclamation to the World
  • Reemphasizes the importance of the family unit as established and ordained by God
  • The Restoration of the Fulness of the Gospel of Jesus Christ
  • Declares to the world that the same church and gospel established by Jesus Christ has been restored to the earth
